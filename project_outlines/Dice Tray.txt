My brother plays a lot of DND (he’s always in at least two campaigns at the same time). So for his birthday, I decided to make him a new dice tray. The one he had was pretty nice, but I had overheard him talking about how the dice would fly out of the tray when rolled, since it lacked walls to catch the dice as they bounced. 


I wanted the tray to keep the same look, so I bought a chunk of walnut for woodturning off the web, and decided to carve a deep-walled hexagon out of it so that it was all one singular piece. It turns out, though, walnut is really hard! So the process took longer than anticipated. 


(trypophobia warning, btw)
