Despite my age, I still have a place in my heart for Pokemon. Out of all of the gazillion pokemon, Torterra has been my absolute favorite. Despite its design being very chaotic and more detailed than most pokemon, I find the combination of the “world turtle” theme with a zen garden aesthetic to be really fun. Also, Torterra is just inherently cool (just look at how many spikes it has!). 


/home/teddy/Documents/website/images/0389Torterra.png


So one day, it hit me. What if I made Torterra into a bonsai planter? Bonsai are essentially just miniature trees tortured enough to keep them small, so that could be a really cool effect, I thought in my head. At the time I’d been really inspired by a youtuber (< link to https://www.youtube.com/sculpturegeek) who carved with plasteline clay, so I bought some Monster Clay online and went to work. 


Early on, I discovered that while the clay was great to work with, it would not be strong enough without a base. It needed to be suspended so that I could access the bottom, but without a base any attempted method would tear the clay. 


I used the 3d printer at my internship to print a simple oval, with a section cut out. I then modeled the turtle around this. What followed was a long process of trial and error. I would sculpt the head, then the legs, then the shell, and by that time my ability had gotten good enough that the head looked bad and I had to resculpt, rinse and repeat.

At some point, I realized that the sharp, geometric edges of the shell were really hard to make with clay, especially the overhang that hangs out over the actual body of the turtle. So I bought a 3d printer (the next logical course of action) and made a new shell.

This is not a finished project, as my standards have grown beyond my skills. If I were to keep going, I think I want to switch to a wax medium instead of clay, as it has less of a chance of getting nicked during the carving process (which causes a lot of unnecessary repairs). The funny thing, though, is that anyone else would say that it’s good enough. Who knows, maybe I’ll give in and finally mold and cast what I have so it can finally fulfill its destiny.
