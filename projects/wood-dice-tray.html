Wood Dice Tray

My brother plays a lot of DND (he’s always in at least two campaigns at the same time). For his birthday, I decided to make him a new dice tray. The one he had was pretty nice, but I overheard him talking about how the dice would fly out when rolled, since it lacked walls to catch the dice as they bounced.

I wanted the tray to keep the same look, so I bought a chunk of walnut for woodturning and decided to carve a deep-walled hexagon out of it so it would be one single piece. It turns out, though: walnut is really hard. So the process took longer than anticipated.
