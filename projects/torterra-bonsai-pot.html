Torterra Bonsai Pot

Despite my age, I still have a place in my heart for Pokémon. Out of all of the gazillion Pokémon, Torterra has been my absolute favorite. Even though its design is chaotic and more detailed than most, I love the combination of the “world turtle” theme with a zen garden aesthetic. Also, Torterra is just inherently cool (just look at how many spikes it has!).

So one day it hit me: what if I made Torterra into a bonsai planter? Bonsai are essentially just miniature trees tortured enough to keep them small, so that could be a really cool effect. Around that time I’d been really inspired by a YouTuber, Sculpture Geek, who carves with plasteline clay, so I bought some Monster Clay online and went to work.

Early on I discovered that while the clay was great to work with, it wouldn’t be strong enough without a base. It needed to be suspended so I could access the bottom, but without a base, any attempted method would tear the clay.

I used the 3D printer at my internship to print a simple oval with a section cut out. Then I modeled the turtle around it. What followed was a long process of trial and error: I’d sculpt the head, then the legs, then the shell, and by that point my skill had improved enough that the head looked bad—so I’d resculpt. Rinse and repeat.

At some point, I realized the sharp, geometric edges of the shell were really hard to make with clay—especially the overhang that hangs out over the turtle’s body. So I bought a 3D printer (the next logical course of action) and made a new shell.

This isn’t a finished project—my standards have grown beyond my skills. If I keep going, I think I want to switch to a wax medium instead of clay, since it’s less likely to get nicked during carving (which causes a lot of unnecessary repairs). The funny thing is: anyone else would probably say it’s good enough. Who knows—maybe I’ll give in and finally mold and cast what I have so it can fulfill its destiny.
