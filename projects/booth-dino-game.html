Parody of Chrome Dino Game

You can play the game in the browser here: https://one-kappa-tea.itch.io/totally-just-the-chrome-dino-game

When it hit the second year of booth, I knew I wanted to take it up a notch and make a full‑on video game. I didn’t have prior game‑design experience, so I had to learn everything from scratch. Unfortunately I don’t have many photos of the actual development, so I won’t go too deep into that process here. I also did just as much physical booth-building as I did the previous year, but the process is the same as last years, so if you want to see more details, check out the Booth Construction + Marble Maze project.

The game is a parody of the Chrome Dino game that appears when you’re offline in Chrome. Since it’s a parody, our “twist” was that this would be the extinction event: all the dinosaurs are running away from a barrage of meteors. To drive this home, the original dino is killed in the intro cutscene, and it’s revealed that the real player character is the school’s mascot, Scotty. To make it more intuitive and interactive, the player jumps in the game by physically jumping in real life via a pressure pad and an Arduino.

Technically, the game wasn’t too complex: it’s a simple side‑scroller with a spawner for enemies and cacti off on one side. Most of the time went into animations and effects—the starting and ending cutscenes, plus all the animated dinosaurs in the background. The project ended up being a great balance: challenging enough to push me to learn new skills, but achievable enough that I felt steady progress throughout.

To my surprise, people really loved it. We had a long line coming out of the booth for the entire first day of Carnival (which might not have been solely due to the game—the rest of the builders did an amazing job on the booth itself).

Unfortunately, despite everyone’s best efforts, we didn’t place in the booth awards that year. Mark my words, though—I will get our booth to place…
